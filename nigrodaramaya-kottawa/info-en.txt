This Nigrodharamaya is located near the village of Kottawa in the Colombo District of the Western Province. Two hundred years ago, a non-sectarian ascetic who had ordained himself lived there. After the death of the last ascetic who lived there, a board of trustees governed it until 1996 and appointed various monks as the incumbent of the temple from time to time at their invitation, and since then the current incumbent has been leading it.
Currently, there is no board of trustees. All activities are carried out under the guidance of the incumbent. Since ten years ago, the current incumbent has been committed to ensuring that all activities are carried out properly.
In addition to the Asathu Bodhi, there is also an ancient Nuga Bodhi. There is an ancient lamp post (lamp house) and in the past, the light at night was provided by the coconut oil lamps lit in that lamp house. The old paintings here belong to the Kotte period.
There is a Dhamma school called Sandavimana in this temple. About four hundred Buddhist children are currently receiving Dhamma knowledge there. It is a very valuable old temple located in the Western Province.
